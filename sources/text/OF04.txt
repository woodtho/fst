4.3 – GRAMMAIRE

PRONOMS PERSONNELS
There are several ways of expressing ownership in French. Here are two of them.

1. A subject pronoun and the verb “avoir” (to have).
J’ai un livre.
I have a book.

2. The verb “être” (to be) followed by an object pronoun.
C’est à moi.
It belongs to me.

Note: Two types of pronoms are used.

Subject pronouns
Je Tu Il/Elle
On Nous Vous Ils/Elles

Object pronouns
moi toi lui/elle
nous nous vous eux/elles

ADJECTIFS POSSESSIFS

In French as in English, another way of expressing ownership is to use possessive adjectives.

C’est mon bureau.

It is my desk.

However, in French the adjective agrees in gender and number with the object owned: bureau (m. s.) = mon/ton/son bureau, regardless of whether the owner is male or female.
