FORMATION DU PASSÉ COMPOSÉ
The passé composé is formed by adding a past participle to an auxiliary verb (avoir or être) in the present tense.

J’ Tu Il/Elle On Nous Vous Ils/Elles

With avoir
ai as a a avons avez ont

mangé mangé mangé mangé mangé mangé mangé

Je Tu Il/Elle On Nous Vous* Ils/Elles

With être
suis es est est sommes êtes sont

tombé(e) tombé(e) tombé(e) tombé(e)s tombé(e)s tombé(e)s tombé(e)s

* Le vous de politesse est singulier : Vous êtes tombé(e). The following verbs are formed with être:

arriver partir rester
entrer sortir
naître mourir

passer
aller venir retourner (to go back)
monter descendre tomber

Note:

The list of verbs above describe a change in place, position or state, except for rester, which is the opposite of partir.
All of the reflexive verbs, as you will see further on, are also formed with être (se présenter, se rendre, etc.).
When used with être the past participle agrees in gender and number with the subject in the same way as an adjective does.
All the other verbs take the auxiliary avoir.

USAGE DU PASSÉ COMPOSÉ
We generally use the passé composé to express a past event happening at a definite time or having a specific duration. It is mainly used to describe a series of events in a narration.
Le colis est arrivé ce matin. Je ne l’ai pas ouvert tout de suite.
The package arrived this morning. I didn’t open it right away.
J’ai attendu trois heures.
I waited three hours.

USAGE DE L’IMPARFAIT

We generally use the imparfait

1. to express habits or situations that no longer exist. 2. to express an action in progress, that was going
on.

1. L’année dernière, avant l’incendie, je mangeais à ce restaurant chaque jour.

In the past, people wrote / used to write with typewriters.

2. Hier, je regardais la télévision au moment de l’explosion.
Last night I was watching television when the explosion happened.

USAGE DU PASSÉ COMPOSÉ ET DE L’IMPARFAIT
We generally use the passé composé to express a definite event in the past. However, we normally use the imparfait to describe the context of that event.
Micheline est sortie à 7 h. Il faisait beau.
Micheline went out at seven o’clock. It was nice.
Il faisait beau quand Micheline est sortie.
It was nice when Micheline went out.
